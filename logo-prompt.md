# ClaimFlow AI — Logo Generation Prompt

## Prompt (use in Midjourney, DALL-E, or Ideogram)

```
Minimalist tech startup logo for "ClaimFlow AI", a healthcare AI company.

Design: A clean geometric mark combining a stylized upward-flowing arrow or pulse line emerging from a shield or document shape. The arrow should feel like both a medical heartbeat line and an upward financial recovery trend. The form should be abstract, modern, and instantly readable at small sizes.

Style: Flat vector, no gradients, no shadows. Swiss/Bauhaus-influenced precision. Similar design language to Stripe, Linear, or Notion — quietly confident, zero decoration.

Color: Primary mark in pure white or #00D37F (bright emerald green) on a near-black background (#080C10). The logomark and wordmark should work together and independently.

Wordmark: "ClaimFlow" in a clean geometric sans-serif (similar weight to Inter or Neue Haas Grotesk). "AI" as a small badge or superscript in the accent green.

Do NOT include: red crosses, stethoscopes, hearts, pill icons, plus signs, or any obvious medical clichés. This is a tech company that happens to work in healthcare — not a hospital.

Format: Square artboard, centered logomark above wordmark, generous white space. Output should feel like it belongs next to Stripe, Brex, or Plaid — premium fintech energy in a healthcare context.

Mood: Precise, trustworthy, quietly powerful. Zero noise.
```

## Variations to try

1. **Logomark only** — just the shield/arrow mark, no wordmark. Used as app icon, favicon.
2. **Horizontal lockup** — mark left, wordmark right. Used in nav bars.
3. **Dark background version** — white/green mark on #080C10.
4. **Light background version** — dark mark on white/light gray.

## Color Palette

| Token | Hex | Use |
|-------|-----|-----|
| Brand Green | `#00D37F` | Primary accent, CTA buttons, logomark highlight |
| Near Black | `#080C10` | Background, dark mode base |
| Off White | `#F0F4F8` | Primary text, light version mark |
| Muted Blue-Gray | `#8A9BB0` | Secondary text |

## What to avoid

- Medical clichés (crosses, pills, stethoscopes)
- Gradient-heavy logos
- Overlapping letterforms or complex monograms
- Anything that reads as "insurance company" or "hospital"
- Sans-serif with rounded terminals (too friendly — needs precision)
